Here I write my first post :000

When I first looked into the brief, it was important for me to think of commmunity bases in Sydney that may need help with growing members or connecting with others outside of just social media. With the emergence of sport based clubs, the group was similarly interested in badminton. We found the current scene for badminton is quite low, whilst fitness clubs like run and oz tag were quite commonly seen on Instagram, we wanted to help with growing people's interest for social badminton. With some research, we found badminton to be enjoyed between friends with social clubs running between acquaintances.


To brainstorm specific features, we created a user persona for our group to help us narrow down and think of specific features that would be helpful to the demographic. We wanted to design for a user that wants to network whilst also getting better at their skills in badminton, someone that isn’t too beginner but rather a person who wants to upscale their skills whilst also meeting new people. We found that looking at current forms of social clubs include facebook groups, meetup websites or instagram pages helped us brainstorm gaps in the market. 


We found that a website that allowed users to join social clubs without the need of contacting the organiser would help a lot of people feel less overwhelmed. There is usually a skill check that the organiser asks for each person which can be intimidating for users to confirm. We were thinking of ways a social club can be joined without that barrier. 


We also were brainstorming ideas for users to check badminton courts, as google maps can be limited in information/reviews we were thinking of putting price, parking, PT rating, availability etc for each court for any badminton user to check. Whilst the website doesn’t need to be limited to social club joiners, we found that providing resources for people interested would help them a lot get into it without any information barriers.  


Whilst our group hasn’t confirmed specific features, I found using a figma board to collaborate with my teammates to be useful in understanding interactions in ensuring we have a common understanding of our website's features. Next week we should be finalising our final product with sitemaps created next week, which will allow us to delegate tasks within the upcoming weeks. A sitemap would be useful in understanding the users interactions ensuring that it flows nicely.
